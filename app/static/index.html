ℹ️ Getting Started (click to expand) ▶

This tool predicts when your boat has enough water to safely leave or return to a swing mooring in Langstone Harbour.

Quick start (no account needed)

Enter your boat's draught and an estimate of your mooring's drying height below, then click UKHO (7 days) and then Calculate Access Windows to see your access windows. Alternatively you can also paste data from the Royal Navy KHM Portsmouth tide tables (linked to below) by clicking KHM Paste for a longer range, or use the built-in harmonic model for an approximate long-range forecast.

KHM predictions are more accurate than harmonic predictions, and UKHO predictions are likely more accurate than both. The secondary port offset for Langstone Harbour is automatically applied for data pasted from the KHM tide table.

Saving your configuration

Enter a Mooring ID (1–100) and click Save Configuration to persist your settings. When re-visiting the page in future, or if you need to revise any aspect of the configuration, enter your mooring ID and press Load from Mooring ID to populate the tool with your last saved configuration.

This also enables the iCal subscription feed — a URL you can add to Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, or Outlook so calculated access windows appear automatically on your phone or desktop. The feed will start with the access windows you initially calculate, and then will update daily at 02:00 BST. During the update, UKHO data is used to predict access windows for the next 7 days. If data is needed for further into the future, either use the KHM Paste option or the Harmonic (long range) prediction. Your UKHO-calculated data for the next 7 days is never overwritten by these alternative sources.

Improving accuracy

If you are using a mooring ID, you can enable Use Observational Data to provide observational information as to whether your boat is afloat or aground at specific times. Over time, these observations narrow down the true drying height of your mooring and a confidence rating is shown. Observations can be entered manually or batch-imported from a spreadsheet that you can fill in over time.

Wind offset

If your mooring is near the edge of the channel, the direction the boat lies as the tide drops affects the depth available. Provide the direction in which the shallower-water lies, and the additional extra height of the ground in that direction have access windows automatically adjusted for when the wind may have pushed the boat toward the shallow side. During each tide's ebb, the system checks which direction the wind is blowing and updates the access window if it's likely the boat is lying on dryer/shallower ground.

PIN protection

Each mooring configuration is protected by a 6-digit PIN. The first time you save a mooring, you will be prompted to set a PIN — keep a note of it, as it is required for any future changes to that mooring's configuration, observations, or calendar feed. The PIN is cached for the duration of your browser session so you will not be asked repeatedly. If you forget your PIN, the person who manages the server can reset it for you.

Obligatory safety warning

This tool is provided with absolutely no guarantee of accuracy and may have limited value over simply assuming you can use your boat around 2 hours either side of High Water. It should not be relied upon for navigation, safety or any other critical purpose.

Tides at Langstone Harbour
Subscribable tide feeds
7-day UKHO: Langstone_UKHO_7d.ics
180-day extended: Langstone_Harmonic_180d.ics
The 7-day feed is UKHO Admiralty data only. The 180-day feed uses UKHO for the next 7 days and the built-in harmonic model for days 8–180; harmonic events are marked “est.” in their title and are accurate to roughly +/-15-20min on time and +/-0.15m on height. Both feeds refresh daily at 02:00.

This tool provides tidal and weather information for general planning purposes only. Predictions are based on modelled data and may not reflect actual conditions as height of tide can vary significantly due to wind and atmospheric pressure. It should not be relied upon for navigation, safety-critical decisions, or any purpose where inaccurate data could result in harm. Always verify conditions against your own observations and judgement before going afloat.
